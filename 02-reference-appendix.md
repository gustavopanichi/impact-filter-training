# Impact Filter — Reference Appendix

This is the detailed reference for trigger criteria, SME logic, exclusions, and calibration. The project instructions define the output format and core rules. If anything here conflicts with the instructions, the instructions win.

---

## A. The six basics

For every intake, extract these six fields and tag each as ✅ Confirmed, ⚠️ Provisional, or ❌ Missing.

### 1. Budget range

Look for: dollar amounts, ranges, tier language such as "small project," "major investment," "flagship," "priority."

If missing: note that SME threshold-based exclusions ($50k, $30k, $25k, $5k) cannot be confirmed.

### 2. Visibility level

Look for: internal-only, executive leadership, board, customer-facing, investor-facing, launch audience, conference audience, external exposure.

If missing: note that high-impact visibility assessment may be provisional.

### 3. Deliverables and formats

Look for: what is being produced, channels, number of assets — decks, websites, social, print, video, etc.

### 4. Timeline and urgency

Look for: deadlines, launch dates, event dates, urgency language.

### 5. Level of clarity

Assess whether: goals are defined, audience is defined, messaging is approved, brand guidance exists, stakeholders appear aligned, direction is exploratory or conflicting.

### 6. Requested capabilities

Look for: design, content, UX/digital, brand/identity, strategy, AI-assisted work, production, accessibility, rollout.

### Discovery question examples

When a field is missing or provisional, generate a client-friendly follow-up question. Examples:

- Budget → "Can you share an approximate budget range so we can scope appropriately?"
- Visibility → "Who's the primary audience for this work? Will it be seen by executive leadership or external audiences?"
- Clarity → "It sounds like the direction may still be evolving — is there approved messaging or brand guidance we should follow, or are we starting from scratch?"
- Capabilities → "Can you confirm which disciplines you'd like us to cover — strategy and messaging, or design and production only?"

## B. Trigger assessment

Evaluate all triggers regardless of how complete the six basics are. Tag each as Confirmed, Provisional, or Not detected.

When a trigger depends on a field that is missing, auto-tag it as Provisional.

### 1. High-impact visibility

Mark active only if explicitly present:

- executive, C-suite, board, or investor audience
- rebrand, visual identity, or brand refresh
- brand-defining or flagship campaign
- first campaign or first launch in a new direction
- major conference or global launch
- foundational template system or core communications system that is org-wide or brand-wide in scope (not a template for a single team or initiative)

Do not activate only because work is external, customer-facing, public-facing, social, or digital.

**Depends on:** Visibility level, Budget range.

### 2. Strategic in nature or ambiguous

Mark active if:

- narrative, concept, or creative platform must be developed
- goals or audience are unclear
- net-new positioning or messaging is required
- stakeholder inputs conflict
- work cannot proceed well without brand or content strategy decisions

**Depends on:** Level of clarity, Requested capabilities.

### 3. Brand-defining opportunity

Mark active if:

- the work sets a new standard for future work
- the work will scale across many assets
- the work signals a shift in positioning
- the work creates a foundational system future work depends on

**Depends on:** Deliverables, Requested capabilities, Level of clarity.

## C. Complexity flags

These are operational red flags, independent of the strategic triggers above. Tag each as Confirmed or Provisional.

### 1. Unclear scope or missing detail

Activate if: goals, audience, or deliverables are unclear; stakeholder input conflicts; the request cannot be translated into a clear execution plan.

Auto-fire as Provisional if 3+ basics are missing.

### 2. Constrained timeline or budget

Activate if: timeline is tight relative to scope; budget appears too low for ambition; scope × quality × timeline do not align.

Mark Provisional if timeline or budget is missing but concern exists.

### 3. Cross-functional complexity

Activate if: multiple teams or stakeholders are involved; work spans multiple formats or channels; dependencies exist across strategy, creative, digital, production, accessibility; the work combines several disciplines in a non-routine way.

## D. SME signals

SME signals do not determine the path. They identify relevant expertise if escalation is appropriate.

For existing accounts, the DD is always the primary consultant. SMEs are surfaced in addition to the DD only when triggers clearly warrant it or when in doubt.

If budget is missing, threshold-based exclusions are Provisional.

### Gina — Strategic Brand Systems & Experiences
*Defining what a brand is — and how it shows up everywhere it matters*

**Signal if:**
- work defines or evolves the brand
- strategy must be defined before design begins
- narrative or concept development is required
- integrated campaign strategy across channels is needed
- digital or experiential brand strategy is involved
- AI in brand systems is relevant
- strategic priority or budget ≥ $50k

**Do not signal if:**
- work is confirmed under $50k with no strategic signal
- work is templated, executional, or production-only

**PM questions:** Does this define or evolve the brand? Do we need strategy before we design? Does this require a concept across channels? Is this a strategic priority?

### Laura — Content Strategy
*Shaping what a brand communicates*

**Signal if:**
- messaging is new, unclear, or undefined
- content is being created from scratch for a brand or sub-brand
- launch or GTM planning is involved
- a high-visibility narrative is required (exec, keynote, launch)
- content strategy, research, audits, or program design is involved
- AI in content strategy is relevant

**Do not signal if:**
- content work is confirmed below $25k with no strategic signal
- work is only proofreading, editing, or minor copy updates
- the account already has an established content lead or editor embedded — in that case, note "Laura or account content lead" and let the PM decide

**PM questions:** Is messaging unclear or undefined? Is content being created from scratch? Is this a high-visibility narrative?

### Lucía — Visual Brand Systems
*How the brand looks and impacts as a scalable visual system*

**Signal if:**
- a new brand visual direction is needed
- the work must scale across multiple assets as a system
- a visual system is being created or evolved
- campaign visual concepts or brand evolution is required

**Do not signal if:**

**PM questions:** Do we need a new visual direction? Does this need to scale across multiple assets? Are we creating or evolving a visual system?

## E. Calibration examples

### Usually A
- resize assets in an existing template
- minor content swaps with clear instructions
- reformat a deck into an approved template
- export or clean up files in an existing system

### Usually B
- new asset type inside an existing system
- mixed stakeholder input on an otherwise executable project
- short timeline with unclear audience or scope
- mostly executable work that still needs quick alignment

### Usually C
- net-new brand narrative
- executive keynote with unclear message
- flagship campaign or first major launch in a new direction
- foundational org-wide template system or core communications system
- full rebrand or visual identity
